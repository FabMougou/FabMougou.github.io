Computer Science in Healthcare

Clinical Scientific Computing

Clinical scientific computing includes maintaining and running the IT systems and Medical aperatus that helps steamline the healthcare proccess, resulting in a more efficient healthcare system that can effectivly treat more people in less time with fewer mistakes.From online medical records which help doctors and nurses track the treatment their patients and decrease diagnosis times to revolutionary peices of technology such as the "Da-Vinci Machine", allowing for more complex surgeries, with fewer complications, the positive impacts of computer science are no more visible that in healthcare.

With the recent rise in Artificial Intelligence, computer sceince can once again proove itself to be evermore useful in getting patients out of the hospital doors as fast as possible. AI is used to collect extreme amounts of medical information and use that to accuratly aid the diagnosis proccess. Recent events, like the Covid-19 also has given the opportunity for computer sceince to help, with the use of the Covid-19 tracking app used to detect and measure the spread of the virus in real time.

The Issues it Faces

With the lives of actual people depending on the reliability of these systems and machines, the effects if something goes wrong could be catastrophic. In 2017, the globe was struck by a high-scale ransomware attack which affected over 150 countries including the NHS in the UK, compromising the integrity of the entire countries medical information. Computer scientists must make sure that their systems are heavily tested and secured. One slight error in the coding of the movement of certain surgucal machinary can result in massive complications for a patient.
